London Wetland Centre

Queen Elizabeth's walk, Barnes, SW13 9WT.

09.00-17.30

Offsite Agenda

For the ODI Q3 all staff offsite we will have the opportunity to escape the city and get closer to nature with a visit to the London Wetland Centre.

The wetlands is famous for its wildlife including the otters! Fingers crossed we’ll have great weather and can make the most of the fresh air. Our visit also includes a one hour tour of the wetlands .

The centre part of The Wildfowl & Wetlands Trust (WWT). They are a conservation charity who work with communities, businesses and governments to help people live sustainably alongside the wetlands. They also work at conserving the variety of wildlife in the area.

Please arrive at the offsite between 09.00 and 09.30 on both Wednesday and Thursday. Once you arrive we will be in the Water's Edge Room.

Drinks & BBQ at the White Hart

The Terrace, Barnes, SW13 0NR.

18.00-22.00

In the evening of the first day we will be having food and drinks at the White Hart pub. Sitting on the riverbank should be the perfect way to end a summer's day. There will be a selection of Canapés for when we arrive and a BBQ running throughout the evening.

Should you wish to order something from the BBQ you can order at the bar using the ODI tab. (the tab will have a password which I'll communicate to you all on the day). We then have a group of tables reserved should you wish to sit and eat (or it rains!!) Otherwise you're welcome to enjoy the outside areas and balconies, and fingers crossed sunshine!

Please aim to get to the Pub as soon as you leave the venue. BBQ starts at 19.00. It is a 1 mile walk to the pub. (If you require any assistance getting there please let me know). Click here to see directions to the pub.

Travel

How do i get there?

  • By Tube

    Hammersmith

    Hammersmith tube (on the Piccadilly and District & Circle Lines) is the nearest tube station. From here it is just a 10 minute bus ride (see ‘by bus’)

  • By Bus

    The 283

    Hop on the number 283 bus direct to the centre from Hammersmith bus station (stand K). Alternatively buses 33, 72 and 209 stop nearby (alight at the Red Lion stop, approximately 150 metres walk from the wetland centre).

    Please note: outside of opening hours (i.e. before 9.30am and after about 5.45pm) the 283 bus terminates nearby in Barnes, rather than coming directly into the centre

  • By Train

    Barnes Rail Station

    Barnes rail station is just 25 minutes from Waterloo or 10 minutes from Clapham Junction. It is then a 15 minute walk to the Wetland Centre (OR 5 minutes by buses 72 or 33).

    You can also alight at Barnes Bridge station then walk through Barnes village (about a 15 minute walk).

  • By Bike

    Cycle route 4

    The London Wetland Centre is situated on Sustrans Cycle Route 4, which includes access from the River Thames towpath. In addition to standard cycle racks there are fifty secure bike cages.

Premier Inn, Hammersmith

255 King Street, London W6 9LU

Those of you spending the night at a Hotel (you should know who you are, however you can check with Joe if you need) will be staying the Premier Inn. It's 2 minute walk from Ravenscourt tube OR a 10 minute walk from Hammersmith tube. Breakfast is NOT included with the room as there will be breakfast at the Wetland Centre.
